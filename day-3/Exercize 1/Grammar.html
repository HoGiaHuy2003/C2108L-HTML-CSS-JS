NOUNS

A noun represents a person, place, animal, thing or an idea.

KINDS OF NOUN

Nouns are classified as follows:

  1. Proper: It is the name of a particular object, whether person, place, animal, idea or thing.
  2. Common: It is the name possessed by things in general.
  3. Collective: It is the name represents a group of things.
  4. Abstract: It is the name represents qualities, emotions, conditions or actions.

PRONOUNS

A pronounis a word used in place of a noun. A pronoun is generally used to avoid repetition of a noun.

KINDS OF PRONOUN

Pronouns are classified as follows:

  1. Personal pronouns: It refers to a specific noun and used as a substitute of the noun.
  2. Interrogative pronouns: It stands as the answer to the question about a person, place or a thing.
  3. Indefinite pronouns: It is used to refer to a person, thing or a place that are unknown.
  4. Relative pronouns: It relates to a noun, pronoun, word or expression preceding it in the sentence, adn at the same time connects two statements.
  5. Demonstrative pronouns: A noun or pronoun is specified and identified by it.
  6. Reflexive pronouns: It usually follows a noun or a pronoun and used to emphasize the subject.
